Benefits of using Tomatoes

At some point in history, the Europeans believed tomatoes to be harmful, given their gleaming appearance. The Aztecs were the principal set of individuals who utilized tomatoes for cooking. With the passing of years, the development spread to Asia. As of today, China and India are the top producers of tomato on the planet.

Tomato is technically considered a fruit but is generally lumped into the category of vegetables as a practice. Daily consumption of tomatoes provides a great boost to health, along with improving the flavour of food.

The following are the benefits of using tomatoes

Eye care -

Tomatoes are rich in vitamin A, which is one reason they are excellent for your eyes. The retinas of your eyes rely on vitamin A, and low levels of the vitamin can, with time, cause blindness. According to research, nutrients like lutein and beta-carotene in tomato support vision and protect against eye conditions including cataracts and macular degeneration.

Other eye-beneficial nutrients in tomatoes are vitamin C and copper. While the former might fight age-related cataracts, the latter helps produce melanin â€“ the important black pigment in the eyes.

Pregnancy -

It is essential before and during pregnancy to protect against neural tube defects in infants.

Reduced blood pressure -

Tomatoes are also rich in potassium, a mineral known to lower blood pressure levels. Potassium eases the tension in the walls of your blood vessels â€“ further reducing blood pressure. But be careful not to consume too much of potassium as it might lead to kidney stones.

They may provide relief from skin problems -

Tomatoes are an important ingredient in most beauty treatments. They help cure large pores, treat acne, soothe sunburn, and recover dull skin. The antioxidants in tomato, especially lycopene, fight cellular damage and skin inflammation.

Urinary Tract Infections -

A recent study states that fresh tomato juice might help in preventing urinary stone formation.

May reduce the risk of diabetes -

Tomatoes are an important part of the diabetic diet. This is because they are rich in iron and vitamins C and E â€“ all of which help relieve diabetic symptoms. A research in Iran concluded that, consuming 200 grams of raw tomatoes every day had a favourable effect on the blood pressure levels in type 2 diabetes patients.

They Improve Digestion -

Tomatoes are good sources of chloride, which is an essential component of digestive juices. Eating antioxidant-rich foods like tomatoes can also help you deal with gastritis, a condition in which the stomach lining is inflamed. The fluid and fibre in tomatoes may be helpful if you're prone to constipation.

Reduce Cholesterol Levels -

As per studies, individuals who consume fresh tomatoes or tomato juice can lower their bad cholesterol levels and increase the levels of good cholesterol. The consumption of raw tomatoes (14 servings a week for a month) can have a beneficial effect on cholesterol levels in overweight women.

Enhance Immune System -

A tomato-rich diet had boosted the functioning of the white blood cells. The lycopene (and its antioxidant ability) in tomatoes might enhance this ability of the white blood cells, which are known for fighting infections.

Protect the Kidneys -

Consuming tomatoes can reduce the risk of gallstones as well as kidney stones.

Reduce Inflammation

Tomatoes consist of a large number of antioxidants may help fight different types of cancer. They are also a rich source of vitamins and minerals and exert a protective effect against cardiovascular diseases.
