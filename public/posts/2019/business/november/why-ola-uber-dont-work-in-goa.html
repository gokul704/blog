Why OLA | Uber dont work GOA?

Ola and Uber are one of the most popular online cab services in India having a huge number of users. As of 2018, Ola company has expanded to a network of more than 10,00,000 vehicles across 169 cities. As of 2019, Uber is estimated to have 110 million worldwide users a 69% market share in the United States for ride-sharing, and a 25% market share for food delivery. Uber has been so prominent in the sharing economy that the changes in industries as a result of it have been referred to as uberization, and many start-ups have described their products as "Uber for X".


Today, you walk into any city in India and you will find quite a few Ola and/or Uber cabs plying the roads – but not in Goa.


What is the issue with Goa?

The story started in February 2014 when Ola and Uber attempted to get authorization from the legislature to work in Goa. However, after these app based rental taxi administrations seeked authorization, the local cab drivers went on an uncertain strike on 24 Feb 2014. More than 7000 vehicles were off road. The strike took a violent turn on 25 Feb with police falling back on lathi-charge, poisonous gas and arrested few taxi drivers. The strike was canceled on 26 Feb after the administration consented to the requests. The Transport minister Sudin Dhavalikar made a statement saying that, “We have no plans to allow Uber and Ola in Goa.” Chief Minister Manohar Parrikar announced that they will introduce app-based services in the present local taxis in Goa. The plan as of date remains on paper.

The local cab drivers charge over the top rates and the rates are not uniform. Certain areas of taxi drivers in Goa have been blamed for demolishing Goa's notoriety for being they cheat clients and have a wild conduct. They are at times referred to as ‘taxi-mafias’ by local people and tourists. These affiliations take steps to take to the streets on simple notice of the introduction of Ola and Uber in Goa.

Tourism being a major industry in the state, even a short strike prompts tremendous misfortunes and inconvenience to tourists. The strike in Feb 2014 caused many tourists to remain stranded on the airport and other locations. Local people say that the administration pretty much every time winds up supporting the taxi drivers as they structure a piece of their vote-bank and can likewise impact numerous votes.

Recently in Jan 2018 these taxi drivers went on strike protesting and again the legislature needed to drop its arrangements. Seeing the solid hold of the nearby local taxis on their services, the introduction of Ola and Uber is by all accounts a long way starting at now.

It’s the monopoly business of the unions and local goons that won’t let this happen soon!

Although, there are many states in India that have local Taxi unions irrespective of the presence of Ola and Uber!
